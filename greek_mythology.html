Legendary Heroes

Mortals who achieved immortality through their deeds

Heracles (Hercules)

The Greatest of Heroes

Born of Zeus and the mortal Alcmene, Heracles was the strongest man to ever live. Driven mad by Hera, he killed his wife and children, and sought redemption through twelve impossible labors.

He slew the Nemean Lion, defeated the Hydra, captured the Golden Hind, cleaned the Augean stables in a single day, and descended to Hades to capture Cerberus. After death, he was granted immortality on Olympus.

12 Labors Nemean Lion Club & Bow

Slayer of Medusa

Armed with winged sandals, a cap of invisibility, and a mirrored shield from the gods, he slew the Gorgon, rescued Andromeda from a sea monster, and founded the city of Mycenae.

Theseus

Founder of Athens

The son of Aegeus (or Poseidon) and Aethra, Theseus is best known for entering the Labyrinth of Crete and slaying the Minotaur, a half-man, half-bull monster that demanded human sacrifice.

With the help of Ariadne's thread, he navigated the maze, killed the beast, and emerged victorious. He later became the king of Athens and a legendary reformer.

Minotaur Labyrinth Ariadne's Thread

Odysseus

The Cunning Voyager

King of Ithaca, hero of the Trojan War, and protagonist of Homer's Odyssey. Known for his intelligence and cunning rather than brute strength, he devised the Trojan Horse that ended the war.

His journey home took ten years, facing the Cyclops Polyphemus, the witch Circe, the Sirens, Scylla and Charybdis, and the wrath of Poseidon. He is the archetype of the wandering hero.
